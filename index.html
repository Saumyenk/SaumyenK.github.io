About

Probing the edges of the Standard Model.

Postdoctoral Fellow · Harish-Chandra Research Institute

I am a theoretical particle physicist working at the Harish-Chandra Research Institute (HRI), Prayagraj. My research sits at the frontier of beyond Standard Model (BSM) phenomenology, with a focus on dark matter candidates and their signatures at high-energy colliders.

My work spans model-building, the study of electroweak symmetry breaking, and the design of collider search strategies — particularly at the LHC and proposed future colliders. I am interested in how novel theoretical constructs leave observable imprints that experiments can test.

Before joining HRI, I completed my doctoral training in theoretical high-energy physics. I enjoy collaborating across the theory-experiment interface and have contributed to studies connecting effective field theories, dark sector portals, and multi-lepton signatures at hadron colliders.

Research

Areas of Investigation

Theoretical high-energy physics at the collider–cosmology interface.

01

BSM Phenomenology

Building and constraining models that extend the Standard Model — including extended Higgs sectors, supersymmetric frameworks, and gauge-extended scenarios. Emphasis on connecting UV-complete theories to low-energy observables and LHC signatures.

Extended Higgs SectorsSUSYEFTElectroweak Symmetry Breaking
02

Collider Physics

Designing and interpreting search strategies for new physics at hadron and lepton colliders. Work includes signal-background discrimination, Monte Carlo simulation, and projections for the HL-LHC and future circular colliders.

LHCHL-LHCMulti-lepton SignaturesMadGraph · Pythia
03

Dark Matter

Studying dark sector portals and WIMP-like candidates, focusing on how relic density constraints, direct detection bounds, and collider signatures together carve out viable parameter space in BSM models.
